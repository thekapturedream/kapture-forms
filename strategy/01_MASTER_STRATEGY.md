# Kapture Forms · Master Strategy

The single doc that frames every other decision.

---

## What we are

Kapture Forms is a **vertical marketplace** of pre-built compliance forms. We are not a form-builder. We are not a SaaS HR product. We are not a generic e-signature tool. We are the *editor-in-chief* of a curated catalogue — the regulator-mapped fields, the audit hash, the export schema, the publisher revenue share — and the platform that sells access in the format the buyer's stack actually consumes.

We sell to two sides:

- **Buyers** — operators in healthcare, HR, finance, legal, education, etc. — who pay £29 one-time or £29/mo to download or run a regulator-shaped form pack.
- **Publishers** — compliance officers, HR consultants, sector specialists — who list their pack and earn 70% of every sale.

The flywheel: more buyers attracts more publishers; more publishers expands the catalogue; bigger catalogue attracts more buyers.

## What we are not

| Not | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| A form-builder (JotForm, Typeform, Google Forms) | We curate, we don't let buyers build from scratch. The point is *pre-engineered*. |
| A SaaS HR product (Bamboo, Breathe) | Those products are workflows. We are the form layer they consume. We export *into* them. |
| A generic e-sign (DocuSign, PandaDoc) | We sign and audit-hash, but the value is the regulator-mapped fields + conditional logic. |
| A compliance consulting firm | We sell artefacts, not hours. Publishers sell hours; we sell their pre-built packs. |
| Free | Price floor is £29. The £0 bracket attracts the wrong buyer and dilutes the publisher payout. |

If a feature pulls us toward any of these categories, kill the feature.

## Why this can win

Three structural advantages:

**1. The compliance-form layer is the most underbuilt part of every UK SaaS stack.** Nobody has built a curated marketplace for it. JotForm and Google Forms are infrastructure plays — anyone can build any form. Kapture Forms is editorial — only the regulator-shaped, audit-hashed, conditionally correct packs make the catalogue.

**2. The publisher economy is unsupplied.** Compliance officers, HR consultants, and sector specialists hand-write the same form pack for every client. They cannot easily turn that work into a product. We are the channel and the platform. 70/30 is generous because it is supposed to be — we want the best publishers writing for us.

**3. The brand is calm and operator-credible.** Kapture is built by operators (Acie, the kooper customer base, a network of compliance contacts). The brand chrome — black + yellow + dot wordmark + audit hash everywhere — reads as serious-but-not-stuffy. The opposite of Typeform's marketing-team-energy.

## The bet

**Buyers will pay £29/mo for a hosted form** rather than £0 for a Google Form, because:
- The audit hash is real (Google Forms can't sign anything)
- The export schema mirrors what their HRIS imports (Google Forms is a CSV of column-A-question column-B-answer)
- The conditional logic mirrors how the role actually works (Google Forms has none)
- The brand chrome makes the candidate's first touch feel premium (Google Forms looks like a free Google Form)
- One regulator update later, the pack auto-updates (Google Forms requires the operator to redo it)

If buyers reject this bet, the product is wrong, not the pricing. We don't drop below £29 — we rebuild the bet.

## The defensive moat

Three layers protect the marketplace once it grows:

**1. Editorial curation.** Every pack passes editorial review. We say no. The catalogue stays sharp. Competing on volume (any form, any quality) is a race to the bottom; we compete on trust.

**2. Audit-hash infrastructure.** Every submission gets a hash, time-stamped, signed by the operator's identity. CQC, FCA, EHO inspectors verify against the hash. This is not a feature buyers can replicate in Google Forms. It's our infrastructure.

**3. Publisher retention.** 70/30 is generous. Plus monthly payouts on the 1st with no minimum hold. Plus free DPA + indemnity templates. Plus free editorial mark-up. Publishers stay because the platform pays them well *and* makes their work better.

If a competitor undercuts on price, we add a publisher feature. If a competitor undercuts on commission, we add an editorial feature. We never undercut on price — that's the moat.

## Decisions that drive everything else

1. **Catalogue, not infrastructure.** When in doubt, curate. When tempted to add a "build your own" feature, ask whether it weakens the editorial brand. It usually does.
2. **One pack, six exports.** Every pack ships PDF + DOCX + HTML + CSV + Google Forms + Hosted. Never a subset. Never a seventh format.
3. **Healthcare first, but not healthcare-only.** The Phase 1 ship is healthcare because we have the warm channel (kooper) and the deepest compliance know-how. By Q1 2027, healthcare must be ≤70% of revenue or we are too narrow.
4. **70/30 publisher share is permanent.** Discounts during launch are time-bound. The steady-state share is 70/30. Nobody negotiates.
5. **Audit hash on every submission.** No exceptions, no products without it, no free tier that skips it.

These five decisions answer 80% of future product debates without a meeting.
